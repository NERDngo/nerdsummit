Virtual summit information

How does this virtual NERD Summit work?

March 12, 2020

DRAFT! NO DECISION MADE YET! Decision coming on March 12.

NERD Summit is going virtual!

Dear 2020 NERDs,

Due to the COVID-19 situation we have decided to take the 2020 NERD Summit virtual!

The dates will remain the same, thus the schedule on the website remains unchanged for now.

See more below for details on how this will work.

Why change?

The results of our survey are as follows:

114 responses *

  • 51% Keep the March 2020 dates - I’ll be there (unless I’m sick)!
  • 30% Reschedule to Summer 2020
  • 6% Reschedule to next year, March 2021
  • 13% Go Virtual in March 2020

* last updated 3/10, 8:00am

We feel that the 49% who feel we should do something other than stay the course is significant.

Also, while the risk remains relatively low, we don't feel that NERD Summit is something that is worth any risk to NERD attendees health and well being. You come first, not NERD Summit!

Why pick virtual?

With the decision made not to stick with the in-person conference on March 20-22, the choices for doing a NERD Summit this year are:

  • Reschedule to Summer (month TBD)
  • Go Virtual in March 2020

We felt that virtual had the following advantages over summer:

  • Everyone had already planned to do NERD Summit on these dates
  • Attendees may or may not be able to attend a summer date
  • There is no guarantee that this will not still be an issue in the summer
  • It is extremely doable (more later on that)
  • As Nerds, we feel this would be an interesting experiment!

We know that a few sessions only work in-person and so we will lose those.

How will virtual work?

We will have a Zoom link for each of the "rooms": 135, 145, 221 321, 325 & 329. Plus we will have other "Zoom Rooms" setup for general discussion and help (TBD). This will all be listed on the NERD website.

To attend a session, find the session you want to attend on the website schedule ( nerdsummit.org/#sesssions ) then click the ATTEND link in that session 5 minutes (or less) from the start time. If you join too early, simply wait for the host to arrive.

If you do not already have it, download the Zoom Client for Meetings here: zoom.us/download#client_4meeting (you will be prompted to do so when you click the ATTEND link if you do not have it alreaady).

See this link for more information: support.zoom.us/hc/en-us/articles/201362193-Joining-a-Meeting

For Speakers: join the Zoom session the same way as above. When entering the Zoom session, announce that you are there and the host will allow you to share your screen. Once you are sharing your screen, you are ready to proceed when the session start time arrives.

See this link for more information: support.zoom.us/hc/en-us/articles/201362153-Sharing-your-screen

The sessions are recorded, so if you do not wish to be recorded at all, you may stay muted and turn your video off. The video recording quality should be quite good. We will grab the Zoom recordings and upload them to our YouTube channel.

The host will end the session shortly after the talk ends (ending the Zoom session kicks off the creation of the video file for the session recording).

If you run into issues and need help;

  • Post your issue in #summit channel in the NERD Summit Slack *
  • Email us at support@nerd.ngo

* if you are not on the NERD Summit Slack you can join here: nerd.ngo/join

What about the Hackathon and kids activities on Sunday?

Unfortunately this cannot be done virtually. We will reschedule this for a one-day event during the summer (assuming COVID-19 is under control by then).

We will follow up with more updated information in a later email.

Thank you!
The NERD Team
